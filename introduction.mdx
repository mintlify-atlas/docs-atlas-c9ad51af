ZenTao is a comprehensive, open-source project management platform built specifically for software development teams. First released in 2009, it unifies product planning, sprint execution, bug tracking, quality assurance, document management, and DevOps tooling in a single self-hosted application. Because you run ZenTao on your own infrastructure, your team retains full ownership of its data and workflows — no vendor lock-in, no usage caps.

## Who ZenTao is for

ZenTao is designed to coordinate the three primary roles in a software team: product owners who define requirements, developers who build features, and testers who verify quality. Each role gets a dedicated workspace while sharing a common view of progress, making it easier to track issues and improvements across the entire delivery cycle.

Teams ranging from small startups to large enterprises — including organizations in finance, telecoms, manufacturing, and software development — use ZenTao to improve transparency, streamline communication, and increase delivery efficiency.

## Management methodologies

ZenTao is built around three industry-standard methodologies. You choose the model that fits your team's way of working, and ZenTao adapts accordingly.

## Core concepts

Understanding how ZenTao organizes work makes everything else easier to follow. The platform uses six primary objects, each with a distinct role.

### Products

A **Product** represents a deliverable your team builds and maintains. Products contain the backlog of user stories and are managed by product owners. You can run multiple products in parallel, each with its own set of stories, plans, and releases.

### Projects

A **Project** organizes the people, time, and resources needed to complete work. Projects are linked to one or more products and provide the management layer above executions. A project can span multiple sprints or phases.

### Stories

A **Story** is a user requirement attached to a product. Stories capture what needs to be built from the user's perspective. During project planning, stories are pulled from the product backlog and assigned to an execution (sprint or phase) to be built.

### Tasks

A **Task** is the unit of developer work within an execution. Tasks are broken out of stories, assigned to individuals, and tracked with estimated and actual hours. They progress through states from "not started" to "done."

### Bugs

A **Bug** represents a defect found during testing or by users. Bugs go through a lifecycle: opened by a tester, confirmed, assigned to a developer, resolved, and then verified closed. Bug tracking ties directly to products and executions.

### Executions

An **Execution** is the time-boxed unit of work within a project — a sprint in Scrum terms, or a phase in Waterfall. Executions contain the stories and tasks your team commits to completing within a defined period. Burndown charts and progress reports are generated at the execution level.
