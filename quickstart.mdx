---
title: "ZenTao quickstart: from login to your first sprint"
description: "Walk through creating a product, adding stories, setting up a project, linking stories to an execution, and assigning tasks to start your first sprint."
---

After installing ZenTao, this guide takes you from your first login to a working sprint with tasks assigned and underway. The whole walkthrough takes about 15 minutes. You will create a product, add user stories, set up a project, link the stories to an execution, and create tasks for your team — the complete flow your team will repeat every sprint.

<Note>
  This guide assumes you have already installed ZenTao and can access it in a browser. If not, see the [installation guide](/installation) first.
</Note>

<Steps>

  <Step title="Log in to ZenTao">
    Open your ZenTao URL in a browser. On the login page, enter the admin account and password you set during installation, then click **Login**.

    After logging in, you land on the ZenTao dashboard. The top navigation bar shows the main sections: **Product**, **Project**, **QA**, **Dev**, and **Admin**.

    <Tip>
      If this is a fresh installation and you imported demo data during setup, you will see sample products and projects already in place. You can follow along with those or create your own.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a product">
    A product is the container for all your user stories and requirements.

    1. Click **Product** in the top navigation bar.
    2. Click **Create product** (or the **+** button next to the product list).
    3. Fill in the **Product name** — for example, `Customer Portal`.
    4. Optionally add a description and assign a **Product owner**.
    5. Click **Save**.

    Your new product appears in the product list on the left sidebar. All stories you add next will belong to this product.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add user stories">
    Stories represent the requirements your team will build. Add at least two or three so you have something to plan with.

    1. With your product selected, click **Story** in the left sidebar, then click **Create story**.
    2. Enter a **Story title** — for example, `User can reset their password`.
    3. Set the **Story source**, **priority**, and an estimated number of story points if your team uses them.
    4. Click **Save**, then repeat to add a second story — for example, `User can update their profile picture`.

    Both stories now appear in your product's backlog with a status of **Draft**. Before stories can be linked to an execution, they need to be reviewed and set to **Active**. Open each story, click **Review**, and confirm them as active.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a project">
    A project organizes the people and timeline around delivering the work.

    1. Click **Project** in the top navigation bar.
    2. Click **Create project**.
    3. Enter a **Project name** — for example, `Customer Portal Sprint 1`.
    4. Choose your **Project type**. For a time-boxed sprint, select **Scrum**. For phase-based delivery, select **Waterfall**.
    5. Set the **Begin date** and **End date** for this project.
    6. Link the project to your product by selecting it in the **Products** field.
    7. Click **Save**.

    Your project is created. You will see it in the project list.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create an execution (sprint)">
    An execution is the time-boxed period — a sprint — where the actual work happens.

    1. Open your project and click **Execution** in the left sidebar.
    2. Click **Create execution**.
    3. Enter an **Execution name** — for example, `Sprint 1`.
    4. Set the **Begin date** and **End date** within your project's dates.
    5. Set the **Work days** available for this sprint.
    6. Click **Save**.

    The execution is created with a status of **Waiting**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Link stories to the execution">
    Pull the stories from your product backlog into the sprint so your team knows what they're building.

    1. Open the execution (Sprint 1) and click **Story** in its left sidebar.
    2. Click **Link stories**.
    3. ZenTao shows the active stories in your linked product. Select the two stories you created earlier.
    4. Click **Save**.

    Both stories now appear inside the execution. Their status remains **Active** — tasks are what move them forward.

    <Tip>
      You can also set the story estimate in story points at this stage if you didn't do so earlier.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Start the execution">
    Change the execution's status from **Waiting** to **Started** to signal that the sprint is underway.

    1. On the execution detail page, click **Start** (or change the status dropdown to **Started**).
    2. Confirm the action.

    The execution is now active. Team members can log hours and update task progress against it.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create tasks">
    Tasks are the concrete units of work. Break each story into one or more tasks and assign them to team members.

    1. Inside the execution, click **Task** in the left sidebar, then click **Create task**.
    2. Set the **Task name** — for example, `Build password reset email flow`.
    3. In the **Story** field, select the linked story this task belongs to (`User can reset their password`).
    4. Assign the task to a **Team member**.
    5. Enter the **Estimate** in hours.
    6. Click **Save**.
    7. Repeat to create at least one task for the second story.

    Tasks start with a status of **Not started**. Assignees update them to **Doing** when they begin work and to **Done** when finished.
  </Step>

</Steps>

## What you have now

You have a fully structured sprint:

- A **product** with an active backlog
- A **project** scoping the timeline and team
- An **execution** (sprint) that is started and contains linked stories
- **Tasks** assigned to team members with hour estimates

From here, team members can log in, find their tasks under **My ZenTao**, and begin updating progress. Testers can open bugs against the execution, and product owners can track story completion from the product view.
